This post introduces a longer paper titled **“A Structural Ontology of Physical Reality.”**

The paper does not propose a new physical theory, add new laws, or modify existing equations. It does not compete with general relativity, quantum field theory, or any established formalism. Instead, it works one layer upstream, asking a different kind of question:

*What must be true of reality for physical description to be possible at all?*

The paper treats ontology as a matter of structure rather than substance. It places coherence, constraint, and admissibility as the minimal grammar required for persistence, interaction, time, mass, energy, and information to appear intelligible across representations. Many long‑standing paradoxes are reframed not as failures of physics, but as signals that representational tools are being pushed beyond the regimes in which their assumptions remain admissible.

In this framing, physics is not corrected or displaced. It is clarified.

### Scope and Intent

This work is intentionally non‑operational. It does not generate predictions, experimental proposals, or computational recipes. Its value lies in interpretive stability and conceptual hygiene: reducing category errors, dissolving false paradoxes, and clarifying the relationship between ontology and representation.

Concepts often treated as fundamental—objects, forces, spacetime, particles, information—are repositioned as emergent or representational outcomes of deeper structural constraints. This does not render them illusory. It explains why they work so well where they do, and why they fail gracefully at the edges.

If the paper feels abstract, that is because it operates at the grammatical layer beneath formalism. Mathematics remains indispensable, but it is treated as a tool that operates *within* structure, not as the source of structure itself.

### Relationship to Other Writing

This paper is compatible with other work on coherence, constraint, invariants, and protodomain structure, but it does not require familiarity with that material. It is intended to place concepts cleanly rather than persuade, instruct, or advocate.

If the framing resonates, it is because the structure already holds.

Nothing more is required.
