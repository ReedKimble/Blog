This post introduces a longer paper titled **“Sleep and Attention: Structural Conditions of Interpretive Reset.”**

The paper does not propose a new theory of sleep, introduce novel neuroscience, or revise existing psychological models. It does not compete with biological accounts of circadian rhythm, memory consolidation, or emotional regulation. Instead, it works one layer upstream, asking a different kind of question:

*What structural operation must sleep perform for interpretation itself to remain coherent over time?*

The paper treats sleep not as rest, recovery, or optimization, but as a necessary suspension of attention-driven interpretation. Interpretation enables action, communication, and continuity, but it does so by collapsing structure irreversibly. Over time, uninterrupted interpretation accumulates commitments that cannot be revised from within interpretation itself. Sleep is positioned as the mechanism that temporarily removes interpretive obligation, restoring reversibility before misalignment hardens into fixed belief.

In this framing, sleep is not corrected or redefined. It is relocated.

### Scope and Intent

This work is intentionally non-prescriptive. It does not offer sleep advice, therapeutic guidance, productivity strategies, or institutional recommendations. Its aim is structural clarification rather than intervention.

Concepts such as fatigue, burnout, anxiety, depression, and psychosis are treated not as discrete pathologies, but as predictable regimes that emerge when interpretation is forced to operate continuously without suspension. These regimes are not explained in terms of content, motivation, or intelligence, but in terms of operating conditions and structural constraint.

If the paper feels abstract, it is because it operates at the grammatical layer beneath psychology and neuroscience. Empirical findings remain valid within their scopes, but they are treated as downstream expressions of a deeper requirement: any system that must interpret reality cannot do so indefinitely without pause.
