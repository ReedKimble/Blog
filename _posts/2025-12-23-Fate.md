## Fate (Protodomain)

This post introduces a short paper titled **“Fate (Protodomain).”**

The paper does not treat fate as prophecy, destiny, or cosmic will. It does not appeal to meaning, purpose, or inevitability in the narrative sense. Instead, it places *fate* structurally, as a property that becomes visible once coherence constraints are fully applied.

In this usage, fate names the path space that remains after incoherent alternatives are eliminated. Certain sequences then become unavoidable—not because they are intended or foreseen, but because no coherence-preserving alternatives remain.

Fate, in this sense, is not predictive. It is recognized through instantiation and understood retrospectively. What appears inevitable in hindsight is simply what could not have unfolded otherwise without violating coherence.
